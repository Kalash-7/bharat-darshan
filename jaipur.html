It's a place where history whispers through every street and tradition dances in every color.

Glimpses of Jaipur's History


  • Founded in 1727 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II.
  • Planned according to Vastu Shastra principles.
  • Named after its founder, Jai Singh II.
  • Painted pink in 1876 to welcome the Prince of Wales.
  • Famous for Rajput and Mughal architecture.
  • Became Rajasthan's capital in 1949.
  • Declared a UNESCO World Heritage City.
  • Known as: The Pink City, due to its distinctive pink-colored buildings.

Jaipur's Culture


  • Jaipur's culture reflects a blend of royal heritage, art, and traditions of Rajasthan.
  • It is famous for folk music, dances like Ghoomar, and colorful festivals such as Teej and Gangaur.
  • People wear vibrant traditional clothes and celebrate life with color and joy.
  • The city is known for its handicrafts, jewelry, blue pottery, and block-printed textiles.
  • The popular folk dances are Ghoomar, Kalbeliya, and Kathputli (puppet shows).

Best Time To Visit

Peak Season (October to March)

  • 1. Pleasant and cool weather, perfect for sightseeing.
  • 2. Major festivals like Diwali, Holi, and Jaipur Literature Festival are celebrated.
  • 3. Ideal time to visit forts, palaces, and local markets.
  • 4. Hotels and tourist spots are crowded and slightly expensive.

Moderate Season(July to September)

  • 1. Monsoon brings light rains and greenery to the city.
  • 2. Less crowded, with good discounts on travel and stay.
  • 3. Suitable for enjoying indoor attractions and photography.
  • 4. Occasional humidity but overall a refreshing atmosphere.

Off-Season (April to June)

  • 1. Best for budget travelers and indoor sightseeing.
  • 2. Great deals on hotels and travel packages.
  • 3. Fewer tourists, making it peaceful to explore.
  • 4. Hot and dry weather, with temperatures above 40¬∞C.
