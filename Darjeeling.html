Glimpses of Darjeeling's History


  • Before 19th century: Inhabited by the Lepcha tribe; later ruled by the Kingdom of Sikkim.
  • Late 1700s: The Gorkhas of Nepal captured the region from Sikkim.
  • 1835: The British East India Company acquired Darjeeling from the King of Sikkim on lease.
  • 1840's-1850's: British developed Darjeeling as a hill station and sanatorium.
  • 1850s onward: Establishment of tea plantations; Darjeeling tea gained worldwide fame.
  • Modern Era: Known globally for its tea, tourism, scenic beauty, and cultural diversity.

Darjeeling's Culture


  • A blend of Nepali, Tibetan, Lepcha, and Bengali traditions.
  • Main language: Nepali, but Hindi and English are common too.
  • Famous festivals: Losar, Dasain, Tihar, Buddha Jayanti.
  • Popular food: Momos, Thukpa, and Darjeeling Tea.
  • Folk dances like Maruni and Tamang Selo are popular.
  • People follow Hinduism, Buddhism, and Christianity.
  • Known for handicrafts, woolens, and peaceful lifestyle.

Attractions

Tiger Hill

The most famous viewpoint in Darjeeling, known for its breathtaking sunrise view over Mount Kanchenjunga and even Mount Everest on clear mornings.

Darjeeling Himalayan Railway (Toy Train)

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this charming narrow-gauge train takes visitors through misty hills, tea gardens, and mountain tunnels ‚Äî a must-do experience.

Batasia Loop

A beautifully designed spiral railway loop surrounded by flowers and gardens, offering a panoramic view of the snow-clad Himalayas and a war memorial dedicated to Gorkha soldiers..

Peace Pagoda

A stunning Buddhist stupa built to promote peace and harmony, offering quiet surroundings and a mesmerizing view of the town and mountains.

Padmaja Naidu Himalayan Zoological Park

One of India's best high-altitude zoos, home to endangered species like red pandas, snow leopards, and Himalayan wolves.

Best Time To Visit

Peak Season (March to May & October to November)

  • 1. The most popular time to visit Darjeeling.
  • 2. The weather is clear, cool, and perfect for sightseeing and mountain views.
  • 3. Ideal for visiting Tiger Hill, riding the Toy Train, and exploring tea gardens.
  • 4. Expect a lot of tourists, so book hotels early!

Moderate Season (December to February)

  • 1. The winter months bring chilly mornings and cozy evenings.
  • 2. Great for travelers who enjoy peaceful vacations and cold weather.
  • 3. Snow can be seen on nearby peaks, and the town looks beautiful in mist and fog.
  • 4. Fewer tourists, making it quieter and budget-friendly.

Off-Season (June to September)

  • 1. The monsoon months bring heavy rainfall and lush green hills.
  • 2. Ideal for nature lovers and photographers who love misty landscapes.
  • 3. Some outdoor activities may be limited due to rain and occasional landslides.
  • 4.Hotels offer discounts, making it a good time for budget travelers.

Cuisines Of Darjeeling

Darjeeling momos

Steamed dumplings filled with meat or vegetables, served with spicy chutney.

Thukpa

A hearty noodle soup with vegetables or meat, influenced by Tibetan cuisine.

Darjeeling Tea

World-famous aromatic tea, a must-try beverage.
