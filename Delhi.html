DELHI

Introduction


Delhi — the heart of India — is where history whispers through ancient monuments and modern dreams shine in glass towers.🌄It's a city that blends Mughal charm with metro speed, street food with fine dining, and tradition with technology.Every corner tells a story — from the bustling lanes of Chandni Chowk to the calm of India Gate.Delhi isn't just a place to visit; it's an experience that stays with you forever.

Glimpses of Delhi's History


  • Delhi is believed to be the ancient city of Indraprastha from the Mahabharata.
  • It was ruled by early dynasties like the Tomars and Chauhans.
  • The Delhi Sultanate was founded by Qutb-ud-din Aibak in the 12th century.
  • Mughals made Delhi their capital and built Red Fort and Jama Masjid.
  • The British made New Delhi the capital in 1911.
  • Delhi continues to be a symbol of Indai's political power and cultural diversity
  • After independence in 1947, Delhi became India's capital.
  • Today, Delhi blends ancient history with modern culture.

Delhi's Culture


  • Delhi's culture is a mix of traditional and modern lifestyles.
  • Festivals like Diwali, Holi, Eid, Christmas, and Gurpurab are celebrated with joy.
  • Hindi is mainly spoken, along with English, Punjabi, and Urdu.
  • Delhi is famous for its food like chaat, golgappa, and Mughlai dishes.
  • Delhiites are known for being friendly and energetic.
  • Cultural places like Dilli Haat and India Habitat Centre show local art and traditions.

Attractions

India Gate

A war memorial honoring Indian soldiers, surrounded by beautiful gardens.

Red Fort

A UNESCO World Heritage Site built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan, symbolizing India's history and freedom.

Lotus Temple

The Lotus Temple in Delhi, shaped like a blooming lotus, is a serene place of worship open to all.

Connaught Place

A major shopping and dining hub in the heart of New Delhi.

Humayun's Tomb

A magnificent Mughal monument that inspired the design of the Taj Mahal.

Best Time To Visit

Peak Season (October to March)

  • 1. Best weather — cool and pleasant.
  • 2. Ideal for sightseeing and outdoor trips.
  • 3. Major festivals like Diwali and Christmas.
  • 4. Tourist spots are lively and crowded.

Moderate Season (February to March)

  • 1. Warm to hot weather.
  • 2. Good for short city visits.
  • 3. Indoor attractions preferred.
  • 4. Less tourist rush than winter.

Off-Season (April-June)

  • 1. Very hot weather.
  • 2. Attractions are less crowded.
  • 3. Cheaper hotels and travel.
  • 4. Good for travelers who don't mind the heat.
